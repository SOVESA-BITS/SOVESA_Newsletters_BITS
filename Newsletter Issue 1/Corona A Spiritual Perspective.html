Corona: A Spiritual Perspective

by Maunil (f20190781@pilani.bits-pilani.ac.in)

Any virus causes panic, tension, distress, and fear of death in human society. We all have a basic instinct to save ourselves.

No one wants to die, but everyone who has taken birth must die.

Although this may be common sense, Bhagavad Gita strictly declares the same as seen in Chapter 2 Verse 27: jātasya hi dhruvo mṛtyur ( Translation: For one who has taken his birth, death is certain. )

We may wear masks, apply sanitizer, avoid travels to save ourselves from the clutches of death (here, the Coronavirus), but to escape from death permanently was never possible for any living being.

The great logicians, scientists, and anyone we can name who tried to save themselves or human society from death were never successful. Trying to protect from H1N1 or COVID-19 or any other disease is just a method to save ourselves for a temporary period, but we need to go away soon enough.

By the one thing to ponder, are we really the temporary bodies dying by these pandemics? Or are we something beyond the body? This is what Gītā ( Chapter 2, Verse 17 ) has to offer us in this regard:-

avināśi tu tad viddhi yena sarvam idaṁ tatam vināśam avyayasyāsya na kaścit kartum arhati "Know that which pervades the entire body is indestructible. No one is able to destroy the imperishable soul."

We are not afraid of COVID-19 really, we are afraid of not doing enough before our imminent death catches up with us. We have no real solution for death. Therefore, we often overestimate the time we have left with this body. We can't even say for sure that we will wake up tomorrow. So what is the aim of life? Simply raise the "standard of living" and die alone on a king-sized mattress at the top floor of Burj Khalifa?

Scriptures remind us,

uttiṣṭhata jāgrata prāpya varān nibodhata "Wake up and take advantage of the human form of life."

In Bhagavad Gītā, Lord Kr̥ṣṇa says,

duḥkhālayam aśāśvatam "This material world is temporary and full of miseries."

We are sleeping (i.e. not realizing that we are taking birth and dying again and again), so we are forgetting our real home Vaikuṇṭha.

vigata kuṇṭha iti vaikuṇṭha. It means a place where no anxiety or miseries exist.

Once we reach our real home, which is described in the scriptures as the ultimate destination or param dhāma, we will not come back ( yad gatvā na nivartante)
